Overview

Administering emergency oxygen is a critical life-saving skill that can make a significant difference in a variety of emergency situations. From helping a victim breathe easier to preventing irreversible brain damage, knowing how to effectively use oxygen in an emergency is essential. Additionally, understanding how to safely manage bloodborne pathogens is crucial for preventing the spread of dangerous diseases such as hepatitis, HIV/AIDS, and other life-threatening infections. This course covers both vital skills, following the latest protocols and guidelines from certified industry professionals.

Designed for convenience and flexibility, this course allows you to learn at your own pace. You can complete the training on your schedule, taking the time you need to absorb the material, and typically it will take no more than one hour to finish. Upon successful completion, you will immediately receive a downloadable certification. This certification is valid for two years.

The course curriculum includes the following:

    • Prevention and Protection
    • Universal Precautions
    • Personal Protection Equipment (PPE)
    • Emergency oxygen administration techniques
    • Bloodborne pathogens, including:
      • Hepatitis B and Hepatitis C
      • HIV and AIDS
      • Other potentially infectious materials (OPIMs)
    • Strategies to minimize exposure and infection from bloodborne pathogens
