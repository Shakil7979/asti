Overview

Our CPR/AED and First Aid courses are available in a comprehensive combined training program. This course prepares you to effectively respond to a wide range of emergency situations, providing cardiac care or first aid until professional medical responders arrive. Whether you’re seeking initial certification or renewing your existing credentials, this combined course is the ideal solution.

This course is designed for flexibility and convenience, typically taking 1.5 to 2 hours to complete. You can learn at your own pace, with the ability to pause and resume the training as needed to accommodate your schedule. Upon successful completion, you will gain immediate access to download your wallet cards and certificate. This certification is valid for two years.

This comprehensive course covers the following key curriculum topics:

  • An introduction covering how CPR and prompt first aid care saves lives
  • Overview of Good Samaritan Laws
  • Universal precautions for provider safety
  • Key emergency action steps
  • The provider’s role in the chain of survival
  • CPR techniques for adults, children, and infants
  • Compression-only CPR for when a breathing barrier is unavailable
  • Proper use of an AED for adults, children, and infants
  • Protocols for responding to conscious and unconscious choking
  • Techniques for placing a victim in the recovery position
  • Providing first aid for a variety of conditions, including:
  • Bleeding
  • Chemical injuries
  • Shock
  • Broken bones
  • Poisoning
  • Stroke, heart attack, and seizures
  • Sudden illnesses
  • Heat and cold related illnesses
  • Bites, stings, and burns
